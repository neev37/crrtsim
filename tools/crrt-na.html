How this works

Sodium in CRRT follows simple first-order kinetics. The clearance for Na ≈ the total effluent rate (sieving coefficient ≈ 1).

Serum Na approaches the dialysate Na exponentially:

Na(t) = Nafluid + (Na0 − Nafluid) × e−t/τ

where the time constant τ = TBW / Qeff (use compatible units — L and L/h).

Why this matters. With chronic hyponatremia, the goal is <8 mEq/L correction in 24 h. Standard dialysate (Na 140) corrects far too fast on a patient at Na 115. A custom dialysate of 125 closes the gap slowly — and you can step it up day by day.

The trick. The asymptote is the fluid Na itself. Pick a fluid Na that, even if left running indefinitely, won't overshoot your 24-hour goal. The simulator shades the safe band.
