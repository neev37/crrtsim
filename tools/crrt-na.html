How this works

The model. Sodium follows single-pool first-order kinetics:

[Na]t = Naf + ([Na]0 − Naf) · e−DNa · t / TBW

Naf is the fluid [Na+K] (the asymptote serum Na approaches). DNa is sodium dialysance.

Why DNa isn't just Qeffluent. Pre-filter replacement fluid dilutes the blood entering the filter, so the effluent carries Na at a lower concentration than the patient's serum. The Yessayan group's correction is:

DNa = QB × (QpreRF + QpostRF + QD + QUF) / (QB + QpreRF)

The penalty factor QB / (QB + QpreRF) is invisible at the bedside but can reduce real Na clearance by 10–20%. In CVVHD with no pre-RF it disappears.

The Yessayan playbook. A standard 140-Na dialysate corrects a patient at Na 115 catastrophically fast (Δ > 15 mEq/L in 24 h). Customize a low-Na bag — e.g. add 333 mL sterile water to a 5 L bag of Prismasate to reach ~135 mEq/L — so the asymptote sits just above target. Even an unmonitored 24 h run can't overshoot.

Correction limits — pick by risk, not by clock.

Tier24 h capWhen to use

Acute symptomatic10–12 mEq/LDocumented < 48 h: post-op, MDMA, marathon, polydipsia

Caveats the simulator doesn't model. Free water from D5W IVF, urine output (often hypotonic in recovering AKI — silently raises serum Na), tube feeds, citrate-Na load (ACD-A is 224 mEq/L). Re-measure Na at 6 and 12 h and re-run.

Evidence base

Kinetic model: Yessayan L, Yee J, Frinak S, Szamosfalvi B. Management of dysnatremias with continuous renal replacement therapy. Semin Dial. 2021;34(6):472–479. PMID 34169573.

Mixing-paradigm extension: Chiaramonte R, Yee J, Frinak S, Yessayan L. Safely correct hyponatremia with continuous renal replacement therapy: a flexible, all-purpose method based on the mixing paradigm. Physiol Rep. 2023;11(1):e15496. PMID 36602098.

Original protocol: Yessayan L et al. CRRT for the management of acid-base and electrolyte imbalances in AKI. Adv Chronic Kidney Dis. 2016;23(3):203–210. PMID 27113697.

Severe Na correction limits: Sterns RH. Disorders of plasma sodium — causes, consequences, and correction. NEJM. 2015;372:55–65.

Hyponatremia guidelines: Verbalis JG et al. Diagnosis, evaluation, and treatment of hyponatremia: expert panel recommendations. Am J Med. 2013;126(10 Suppl 1):S1–S42.

European guideline: Spasovski G et al. Clinical practice guideline on diagnosis and treatment of hyponatraemia. Eur J Endocrinol. 2014;170:G1–G47.

Edelman relation ([Na+K]/TBW): Nguyen MK, Kurtz I. Quantitative interrelationship between Gibbs-Donnan equilibrium, osmolality of body fluid compartments, and plasma water sodium concentration. J Appl Physiol. 2004;97:1858–1862.
