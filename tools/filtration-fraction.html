How this works

The principle. As plasma is pulled across the membrane, the blood continuing down the filter becomes progressively more concentrated. Past a critical point, red cells and proteins pack so tightly that the filter clots — fast.

The formula.

FF = (Qpre + Qpost + Qnet) / (Qplasma + Qpre)

With Qplasma = Qb × (1 − Hct). Dialysate doesn't appear in FF — it's diffusive, not convective. It still counts toward delivered dose.

Thresholds. Most adult protocols target FF below 20%; 20–25% is caution; above 25% filter survival falls sharply (Joannidis 2007, Tan 2002). KDIGO target effluent dose is 20–25 mL/kg/h delivered (RENAL, ATN trials).

Levers, ranked by impact.

  1. Pre-dilution — raises the denominator without touching dose. Doubles filter life (van der Voort 2005, Tan 2002).
  2. Blood flow — raises plasma flow proportionally. Access-limited.
  3. Shift post → pre — keeps total dose, drops FF.
  4. Lower the dose — last resort; sacrifices clearance for filter life.

The pre-dilution paradox. Pre-dilution saves the filter and reduces solute clearance, by exactly the factor QB / (QB + QpreRF). The simulator shows the trade-off live (Yessayan, Semin Dial 2021).

Evidence base

Filter-life / FF threshold: Joannidis M, Oudemans-van Straaten HM. Clinical review: patency of the circuit in continuous renal replacement therapy. Crit Care. 2007;11:218.

Pre-dilution benefit: van der Voort PHJ et al. Filter run time in CVVH: pre- vs post-dilution and effect of nadroparin. Int J Artif Organs. 2005;28(4):371–377.

Filter-life observational data: Tan HK et al. Factors related to the haemodynamic and biochemical effects of CVVH. Crit Care Med. 2002;30:1518–1523.

Dose targets: KDIGO Clinical Practice Guideline for Acute Kidney Injury. Kidney Int Suppl. 2012;2:1–138.

RENAL trial: RENAL Replacement Therapy Study Investigators. Intensity of CRRT in critically ill patients. NEJM. 2009;361:1627–1638.

ATN trial: VA/NIH Acute Renal Failure Trial Network. Intensity of renal support in critically ill patients with AKI. NEJM. 2008;359:7–20.

Sodium dialysance correction: Yessayan L, Yee J, Frinak S, Szamosfalvi B. Management of dysnatremias with CRRT. Semin Dial. 2021;34(6):472–479.
