How this works

The principle. As plasma is pulled across the membrane, the blood that continues down the filter becomes progressively more concentrated. Past a certain point, red cells and proteins pack so tightly that the filter clots — fast.

The formula.

FF = (Qpre + Qpost + Qnet) / (Qplasma + Qpre)

Where Qplasma = Qb × (1 − Hct). Dialysate flow doesn't appear — it's diffusive, not convective.

The thresholds. Most adult protocols target FF below 20%; 20–25% is a caution zone; above 25% filter survival drops sharply. Pre-dilution is the most powerful lever — it raises the denominator without touching dose.

Levers in order of impact. Pre-dilution ▸ blood flow ▸ post-dilution ▸ net UF. If FF is high, add pre-dilution before you lower the dose.
