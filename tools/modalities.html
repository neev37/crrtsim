Therapy

Modalities & SCUF

Four flavors of continuous therapy — convection, diffusion, both, or neither. Plus a no-fuss net-UF calculator for SCUF.

Convection

CVVH

Continuous venovenous hemofiltration

  • Replacement fluid (pre &/or post)
  • No dialysate
  • Clears middle molecules better
  • Effluent dose = replacement + net UF
Diffusion

CVVHD

Continuous venovenous hemodialysis

  • Dialysate, no replacement
  • Lowest filtration fraction
  • Best when clotting is a worry
  • Effluent dose = dialysate + net UF
Both

CVVHDF

Hemodiafiltration

  • Replacement + dialysate
  • Most flexible — split as you like
  • Small & mid solute clearance
  • Effluent dose = all components
UF only

SCUF

Slow continuous ultrafiltration

  • No solute clearance
  • Volume removal only
  • Diuretic-resistant CHF
  • Effluent = net UF

How fast is too fast?

Refilling rate from the interstitium to the vascular space is roughly 5–10 mL/kg/h. UF rates that outpace this will drop preload faster than the body can refill the circulation, leading to intradialytic hypotension and end-organ injury.

Common bedside framing:

Safe range ≈ ≤ 5 mL/kg/h · Caution 5–10 · High > 10

Higher rates are sometimes necessary (decompensated CHF, anasarca) but earn closer hemodynamic surveillance.
